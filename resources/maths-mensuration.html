📏 Perimeter — Distance Around a Shape

1. What is mensuration & perimeter?

Mensuration is the branch of maths that measures length (perimeter), area and volume of shapes. The perimeter is the total distance around the edge of a flat (2-D) shape — just add up all the sides.

🌟 Real life: the perimeter is the length of fence you'd need to go all the way round a piece of land; the area is how much grass covers it.
Golden rule: keep the units consistent

Length is in cm or m; area is in cm² or m² (squared); volume is in cm³ or m³ (cubed). Always convert to the same unit before calculating.

2. Perimeter formulas

ShapePerimeter
Square (side s)P = 4s
Rectangle (length l, breadth b)P = 2(l + b)
Triangle (sides a, b, c)P = a + b + c
Circle (circumference, radius r)C = 2πr = πd

Use π = 22/7 or 3.142 (whichever the question suggests).

Worked example: Find the perimeter of a rectangle 8 cm long and 5 cm wide.
P = 2(l + b) = 2(8 + 5) = 2 × 13 = 26 cm
Circle example: Find the circumference of a circle of radius 7 cm (π = 22/7).
C = 2πr = 2 × 22/7 × 7 = 44 cm

3. Perimeter of a sector (part of a circle)

A sector is a "slice" of a circle with angle θ at the centre. Its curved edge (arc) plus the two straight radii make the perimeter.

Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr   |   Perimeter of sector = arc + 2r
Worked example: A sector of radius 7 cm has an angle of 90°. Find its perimeter (π = 22/7).
Arc = (90/360) × 2 × 22/7 × 7 = ¼ × 44 = 11 cm
Perimeter = arc + 2r = 11 + 14 = 25 cm

🟦 Area — Space Inside a Flat Shape

1. Area formulas of plane shapes

ShapeArea
Square (side s)A = s²
Rectangle (l × b)A = l × b
Triangle (base b, height h)A = ½ × b × h
Parallelogram (base b, height h)A = b × h
Trapezium (parallel sides a, b, height h)A = ½(a + b) × h
Rhombus / kite (diagonals d₁, d₂)A = ½ × d₁ × d₂
Circle (radius r)A = πr²

Remember: area is always in square units (cm², m²).

2. Worked examples

Rectangle: area of a rectangle 12 m by 5 m.
A = l × b = 12 × 5 = 60 m²
Triangle: area of a triangle with base 10 cm and height 6 cm.
A = ½ × b × h = ½ × 10 × 6 = 30 cm²
Trapezium: parallel sides 8 cm and 12 cm, height 5 cm.
A = ½(a + b) × h = ½(8 + 12) × 5 = ½ × 20 × 5 = 50 cm²
Circle: area of a circle of radius 7 cm (π = 22/7).
A = πr² = 22/7 × 7 × 7 = 22 × 7 = 154 cm²

3. Area of a sector & using the Hero (Heron) formula

Area of sector = (θ/360) × πr²
Sector: radius 6 cm, angle 60° (π = 3.142).
A = (60/360) × 3.142 × 6² = ⅙ × 3.142 × 36 = ⅙ × 113.1 = 18.85 cm²
Triangle with 3 sides known (Heron's formula)
A = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)], where s = (a+b+c)/2
Example: triangle with sides 3, 4, 5 cm.
s = (3+4+5)/2 = 6
A = √[6(6−3)(6−4)(6−5)] = √[6×3×2×1] = √36 = 6 cm²

4. Areas of compound shapes

For an L-shape or any odd shape: split it into simple shapes, find each area, then add (or subtract a cut-out).

💡 Trick: to find the shaded area between two circles or a shape with a hole, do (big area − small area).

🧊 Surface Area & Volume of Solids

1. Volume formulas

Volume = the amount of space a 3-D solid occupies (in cubic units). For any prism (a solid with the same cross-section all through), volume = area of cross-section × length.

SolidVolume
Cube (side s)V = s³
Cuboid (l × b × h)V = l × b × h
Cylinder (radius r, height h)V = πr²h
Cone (radius r, height h)V = ⅓πr²h
Sphere (radius r)V = ⁴⁄₃πr³
Prism (cross-section area A, length L)V = A × L
Pyramid (base area A, height h)V = ⅓ × A × h
🧠 Memory: a cone is ⅓ of a cylinder with the same base and height; a pyramid is ⅓ of the prism it fits inside.

2. Surface area formulas

SolidSurface area
CubeSA = 6s²
CuboidSA = 2(lb + lh + bh)
Cylinder (closed)SA = 2πr² + 2πrh  (curved part = 2πrh)
ConeSA = πr² + πrl  (curved part = πrl, l = slant height)
SphereSA = 4πr²

For a cone, the slant height l is found with Pythagoras: l = √(r² + h²).

3. Worked examples

Cuboid volume: a tank 4 m × 3 m × 2 m.
V = l × b × h = 4 × 3 × 2 = 24 m³
Cylinder volume: radius 7 cm, height 10 cm (π = 22/7).
V = πr²h = 22/7 × 7² × 10 = 22/7 × 49 × 10 = 22 × 7 × 10 = 1540 cm³
Cone volume: radius 3 cm, height 7 cm (π = 22/7).
V = ⅓πr²h = ⅓ × 22/7 × 9 × 7 = ⅓ × 22 × 9 = ⅓ × 198 = 66 cm³
Sphere volume: radius 3 cm (π = 3.142).
V = ⁴⁄₃πr³ = ⁴⁄₃ × 3.142 × 27 = 4 × 3.142 × 9 = 113.1 cm³
Cone slant height: radius 6 cm, vertical height 8 cm.
l = √(r² + h²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 cm

🧩 Circle Facts & Exam-Style Mixed Problems

1. Key circle facts

  • Diameter d = 2r (twice the radius).
  • Circumference C = 2πr = πd.
  • Area A = πr².
  • A whole circle = 360°. A sector uses the fraction θ/360.
Find radius from area: a circle has area 154 cm². Find its radius (π = 22/7).
πr² = 154 → 22/7 × r² = 154 → r² = 154 × 7/22 = 49 → r = 7 cm

2. Total surface area of a solid (worked)

Closed cylinder: radius 7 cm, height 10 cm (π = 22/7).
Curved area = 2πrh = 2 × 22/7 × 7 × 10 = 440 cm²
Two circular ends = 2πr² = 2 × 22/7 × 49 = 308 cm²
Total SA = 440 + 308 = 748 cm²

3. Compound / real-life problems

How many litres does a tank hold? A cuboid tank is 100 cm × 50 cm × 40 cm.
V = 100 × 50 × 40 = 200 000 cm³
1 litre = 1000 cm³, so V = 200 000 ÷ 1000 = 200 litres
Shaded area (circle in a square): a circle of radius 7 cm sits inside a square of side 14 cm. Find the area left over (π = 22/7).
Square area = 14 × 14 = 196 cm²
Circle area = 22/7 × 7 × 7 = 154 cm²
Shaded = 196 − 154 = 42 cm²

⭐ Exam tips

  • Write the formula first, then substitute — examiners award method marks.
  • Check the units: perimeter (cm), area (cm²), volume (cm³). Convert first if mixed.
  • Use the value of π the question implies — 22/7 when numbers are multiples of 7, else 3.142.
  • For a cone, find the slant height l = √(r²+h²) before curved surface area.
  • For odd shapes, split into simple shapes or subtract a cut-out.
