⚛️ PHYSICS

📘 Your projectile cheat-sheet (memorise these 3)

  • Time of flight T = 2u sinθ / g — how long it stays in the air.
  • Max height H = u²sin²θ / 2g — highest point reached.
  • Range R = u²sin2θ / g — horizontal distance. Biggest at 45°; equal for angles that add to 90° (e.g. 30° & 60°).
  • Throughout flight: horizontal speed never changes; gravity only pulls things down. Use g = 10 unless told 9.8.

📘 Heat & waves cheat-sheet

  • Q = mcΔT (heat = mass × specific heat capacity × temperature change). “Heat lost = heat gained” solves mixing problems.
  • Expansion: linear α, area β = 2α, volume γ = 3α. Liquid’s apparent expansion = real − container’s.
  • v = fλ. Open pipe: all harmonics. Closed pipe: odd only (f, 3f, 5f…). Mirror/lens: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u.

🧪 CHEMISTRY

📘 Acids, bases & pH cheat-sheet

  • pH = −log[H⁺], pOH = −log[OH⁻], and pH + pOH = 14.
  • Arrhenius: acid gives H⁺, base gives OH⁻. Brønsted: proton donor/acceptor. Lewis: electron-pair acceptor/donor.
  • Amphoteric metals (Al, Zn, Be, Sn) react with BOTH acids and alkalis to give H₂. Iron does NOT react with alkalis.

🧬 BIOLOGY

📘 Respiration & excretion cheat-sheet

  • Glycolysis → cytoplasm (also where anaerobic respiration happens). Krebs → matrix. ETC/oxidative phosphorylation → cristae (most ATP).
  • Aerobic ⇒ CO₂ + water + ~38 ATP. Yeast (anaerobic) ⇒ ethanol + CO₂. Muscle (anaerobic) ⇒ lactic acid (oxygen debt).
  • Kidney unit = nephron; filtering in Bowman’s capsule; ADH controls water. Birds/reptiles excrete uric acid.

📝 USE OF ENGLISH

📘 Grammar & vocab cheat-sheet

  • “Neither / Either / Each / Every / One of …” take a singular verb (is, has, was).
  • Past obligation = “ought to / should + have + past participle”.
  • Synonym = same meaning; Antonym = opposite. Words like “but / or / ever” usually signal an opposite is wanted.
