📐 PART 1 — Angles & Lines

1. What is an angle?

An angle is the amount of turn between two straight lines (called arms) that meet at a point (called the vertex). We measure the size of the turn in degrees (°). A full turn all the way round is 360°.

The five basic types of angle
NameSizePicture in words
Acute angleLess than 90°A small sharp corner
Right angleExactly 90°A perfect square corner (⌐)
Obtuse angleBetween 90° and 180°An open, blunt corner
Straight angleExactly 180°A straight line — half a full turn
Reflex angleBetween 180° and 360°More than a straight line, less than full turn
🌟 Memory: Acute is a A tiny/sharp angle (small). Obtuse is the fat, open one. If it is bigger than a straight line, it is reflex.

2. Complementary and supplementary angles

These two words are asked constantly, and students mix them up. Learn them cold:

Complementary angles ADD UP TO 90°
Supplementary angles ADD UP TO 180°
🌟 Memory trick: Complementary → C comes before S in the alphabet, and 90 comes before 180. So Complementary = 90°, Supplementary = 180°.
Worked example: Angle A and angle B are complementary. If A = 35°, find B.
B = 90° − 35° = 55°
Answer = 55°.
Worked example: Two angles are supplementary. One is 118°. Find the other.
Other = 180° − 118° = 62°
Answer = 62°.

3. Angles on a line, at a point, and vertically opposite

Three rules you must know by heart
  • Angles on a straight line add up to 180°. If several angles sit side by side on one straight line, they total 180°.
  • Angles at a point add up to 360°. If several angles meet all the way round a single point, they total 360° (a full turn).
  • Vertically opposite angles are equal. When two straight lines cross, they make an X shape — the two angles that face each other across the crossing are equal.
Worked example (on a line): Three angles on a straight line are 40°, x, and 90°. Find x.
40° + x + 90° = 180°
x = 180° − 130° = 50°
Answer = 50°.
Worked example (at a point): Four angles round a point are 100°, 80°, 90° and x. Find x.
100° + 80° + 90° + x = 360°
x = 360° − 270° = 90°
Answer = 90°.
Worked example (vertically opposite): Two lines cross. One angle is 65°. Find the angle vertically opposite it, and the angle next to it on the line.
Vertically opposite = 65° (equal)
Angle next to it on the line = 180° − 65° = 115°
Answers: 65° and 115°.

4. Parallel lines cut by a transversal

Parallel lines are two straight lines that never meet (shown with little arrows). A transversal is a line that cuts across them. Where it crosses, it makes three special pairs of angles:

PairShape clueRule
Corresponding anglesMake an F shapeEqual
Alternate anglesMake a Z shapeEqual
Co-interior (allied) anglesMake a C or U shapeAdd up to 180°
🌟 Memory: F and Z angles are equal; the C/U (co-interior) angles are the odd ones out — they are supplementary (sum 180°).
Worked example (corresponding): A transversal crosses two parallel lines. A corresponding angle is 72°. Find its partner.
Corresponding angles are equal → partner = 72°
Answer = 72°.
Worked example (alternate / Z): One alternate angle is 55°. Find the other alternate angle.
Alternate angles are equal → other = 55°
Answer = 55°.
Worked example (co-interior): Two co-interior angles are x and 110°. Find x.
Co-interior angles add up to 180°
x = 180° − 110° = 70°
Answer = 70°.

🔺 PART 2 — Triangles & Polygons

1. Angle sum of a triangle = 180°

The single most useful fact in all of geometry: the three interior angles of any triangle add up to 180°. It is true for every triangle, big or small.

Angle A + Angle B + Angle C = 180°
Worked example: A triangle has angles 50°, 60° and x. Find x.
50° + 60° + x = 180°
x = 180° − 110° = 70°
Answer = 70°.

2. Exterior angle of a triangle

If you extend one side of a triangle, the angle formed outside is the exterior angle. There is a neat shortcut:

Exterior angle = sum of the two opposite (interior) angles

The two opposite interior angles are the two angles that are NOT next to the exterior angle.

Worked example: A triangle has interior angles 55° and 65° at two corners. Find the exterior angle at the third corner.
Exterior angle = 55° + 65° = 120°
Answer = 120°. (Check: the interior angle there is 180° − 120° = 60°, and 55° + 65° + 60° = 180° ✓)

3. Types of triangle

TypeSidesAngles
EquilateralAll 3 sides equalAll 3 angles = 60°
Isosceles2 sides equalThe 2 base angles (opposite the equal sides) are equal
ScaleneAll 3 sides differentAll 3 angles different
Right-angled—One angle is exactly 90°
Worked example (isosceles): An isosceles triangle has an apex (top) angle of 40°. Find each base angle.
Base angles are equal, and all three add to 180°
Each base angle = (180° − 40°) ÷ 2 = 140° ÷ 2 = 70°
Answer = 70° each.

4. Congruent vs similar triangles

  • Congruent triangles are exactly the same shape AND size — one is a perfect copy of the other (equal angles and equal sides). Same size.
  • Similar triangles are the same shape but different size — like a photo and its enlargement. Their angles are equal and their sides are in the same ratio (proportional).
🌟 Memory: Congruent = Conpy (copy), same size. Similar = same shape, scaled up or down.

5. Polygons — the three key formulas

A polygon is any closed shape made of straight sides. n stands for the number of sides. A regular polygon has all sides and all angles equal.

Sum of INTERIOR angles = (n − 2) × 180°
Sum of EXTERIOR angles = 360°  (always, for any polygon)
Each EXTERIOR angle of a REGULAR polygon = 360° ÷ n
Handy interior-angle-sum table
PolygonSides (n)(n − 2) × 180°
Triangle3180°
Quadrilateral4360°
Pentagon5540°
Hexagon6720°
Octagon81080°
Worked example (find an angle): Find each interior angle of a regular octagon (8 sides).
Sum of interior angles = (8 − 2) × 180° = 6 × 180° = 1080°
Each interior angle = 1080° ÷ 8 = 135°
Answer = 135°. (Shortcut: each exterior angle = 360° ÷ 8 = 45°, so interior = 180° − 45° = 135° ✓)
Worked example (find the number of sides): Each exterior angle of a regular polygon is 40°. How many sides has it?
Each exterior angle = 360° ÷ n → n = 360° ÷ 40°
n = 9
Answer = 9 sides (a nonagon).
Worked example (find sides from interior angle): Each interior angle of a regular polygon is 150°. Find the number of sides.
Each exterior angle = 180° − 150° = 30°
n = 360° ÷ 30° = 12
Answer = 12 sides (a dodecagon).

⭕ PART 3 — Circle Theorems

1. The words you need first

  • Centre — the middle point of the circle.
  • Radius — a straight line from the centre to the edge.
  • Chord — a straight line joining two points on the edge.
  • Diameter — a chord that passes through the centre (the longest chord).
  • Arc — a part of the curved edge.
  • Segment — the region between a chord and an arc.
  • Tangent — a straight line that just touches the circle at one point.
  • Subtended — an angle is subtended by an arc/chord when the two lines from the ends of the arc meet to form that angle.

2. The six circle theorems (learn this table)

#TheoremIn words
1Angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumferenceStanding on the same arc, the angle at the centre is twice the angle at the edge.
2Angle in a semicircle = 90°Any angle drawn on the diameter (in a semicircle) is a right angle.
3Angles in the same segment are equalAngles standing on the same arc, from the same side, are equal.
4Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral = 180°A 4-sided shape with all corners on the circle: opposite angles are supplementary.
5Tangent ⟂ radiusA tangent is perpendicular (90°) to the radius drawn to the point of contact.
6Alternate segment theoremThe angle between a tangent and a chord = the angle in the alternate (other) segment.

3. Theorem 1 — angle at centre = twice angle at circumference

Angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumference (same arc)
Worked example: An angle at the circumference standing on an arc is 40°. Find the angle at the centre on the same arc.
Angle at centre = 2 × 40° = 80°
Answer = 80°.
Worked example (reverse): The angle at the centre is 140°. Find the angle at the circumference on the same arc.
Angle at circumference = 140° ÷ 2 = 70°
Answer = 70°.

4. Theorem 2 — angle in a semicircle = 90°

If a triangle is drawn inside a circle so that one side is a diameter, the angle opposite the diameter is always a right angle (90°).

Worked example: A triangle is drawn in a semicircle. One of the other two angles is 35°. Find the third angle.
The angle in the semicircle = 90°
Third angle = 180° − 90° − 35° = 55°
Answer = 55°.

5. Theorem 4 — cyclic quadrilateral

Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral add up to 180°
Worked example: In a cyclic quadrilateral, one angle is 85°. Find the angle opposite it.
Opposite angle = 180° − 85° = 95°
Answer = 95°.
Worked example: A cyclic quadrilateral has angles 70°, x, 100° and y, where 70° and 100° are one pair of opposite angles. Find x and y.
70° and 100° are NOT opposite here — 70° pairs with y, and x pairs with 100°
x = 180° − 100° = 80°
y = 180° − 70° = 110°
Answers: x = 80°, y = 110°.

6. Theorems 5 & 6 — tangents

Theorem 5 (tangent ⟂ radius): where a tangent touches the circle, the radius drawn to that point meets it at exactly 90°.

Theorem 6 (alternate segment): the angle between the tangent and a chord equals the angle in the alternate segment (the angle at the circumference on the far side of that chord).

Worked example: A tangent touches a circle at point T. The radius OT and the tangent meet at T. What is the angle between them?
Tangent ⟂ radius → angle = 90°
Answer = 90°.
Worked example (alternate segment): The angle between a tangent and a chord is 62°. Find the angle in the alternate segment.
Alternate segment theorem → angle in alternate segment = angle between tangent and chord
Angle in alternate segment = 62°
Answer = 62°.

🧩 PART 4 — Mixed Geometry Problems

1. Quick facts to combine

  • Angles on a straight line = 180°; angles at a point = 360°.
  • Vertically opposite angles are equal.
  • Parallel lines: F angles (corresponding) equal · Z angles (alternate) equal · C/U angles (co-interior) sum to 180°.
  • Triangle angles sum to 180°; exterior angle = sum of the two opposite interior angles.
  • Isosceles triangle: the two base angles are equal.
  • Polygon interior angle sum = (n − 2) × 180°; each exterior angle of a regular polygon = 360° ÷ n.

2. Properties of special quadrilaterals

ShapeKey properties
ParallelogramOpposite sides parallel and equal · opposite angles equal · co-interior angles sum to 180° · diagonals bisect each other
RectangleA parallelogram with all angles = 90° · opposite sides equal · diagonals equal and bisect each other
RhombusA parallelogram with all sides equal · opposite angles equal · diagonals bisect each other at 90° and bisect the angles
SquareAll sides equal AND all angles 90° (a rhombus and rectangle in one) · diagonals equal, bisect at 90°

3. Worked exam-style problems

Example 1 — triangle + straight line: In a triangle, two angles are 48° and 62°. One side is extended to form an exterior angle. Find (a) the third interior angle and (b) the exterior angle at that vertex.
(a) Third interior angle = 180° − 48° − 62° = 70°
(b) Exterior angle = 180° − 70° = 110° (or use 48° + 62° = 110°) ✓
Answers: (a) 70°, (b) 110°.
Example 2 — parallel lines + triangle: Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. An alternate angle is 50°. This 50° angle is one angle of a triangle whose second angle is 60°. Find the third angle of the triangle.
Alternate angles equal → the angle inside the triangle = 50°
Third angle = 180° − 50° − 60° = 70°
Answer = 70°.
Example 3 — isosceles + circle (semicircle): A triangle is drawn in a semicircle, so one angle is 90°. The triangle is isosceles with the two shorter sides equal. Find the other two angles.
Angle in semicircle = 90°, leaving 180° − 90° = 90° for the other two
They are equal (isosceles), so each = 90° ÷ 2 = 45°
Answers: 45° and 45°.
Example 4 — regular polygon + cyclic quadrilateral: (a) Find each interior angle of a regular pentagon. (b) In a cyclic quadrilateral, three angles are 95°, 100° and 85°. Find the fourth angle.
(a) Interior sum = (5 − 2) × 180° = 540°; each angle = 540° ÷ 5 = 108°
(b) The fourth angle is opposite one of the given ones. 85° pairs with 95° (they sum to 180°) ✓, so 100° pairs with the missing angle
Fourth angle = 180° − 100° = 80°
Answers: (a) 108°, (b) 80°.
